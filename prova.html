High-Conflict Divorce

"In a long-term, high-conflict relationship, love becomes the most threatening thing in the room. When it’s most needed, it’s most resisted."

A high-conflict divorce happens when one partner (typically your ex) engages in controlling behavior, harassment, and/or legal manipulation. These situations are incredibly stressful and emotionally draining, leaving you confused, afraid, or even traumatized.

High-conflict individuals often have deep emotional issues that impact their relationships. You might not understand why your ex is acting this way. Though you’ve tried your best to be fair, they may be trying to undermine you legally, financially, and emotionally.

Dealing With A High-Conflict Ex Can Make You Feel Anxious, Depressed, And Hopeless

High-conflict divorces can create chaos by threatening your lifestyle, security, and self-esteem. You may feel humiliated, constantly anxious, or powerless. When you're overwhelmed, it's hard to make smart decisions. Everything seems like a crisis.

Being on the receiving end of seemingly endless drama can become exhausting. If you're dealing with a high-conflict ex, you may be experiencing:

  • Sleep disturbance
  • Chronic anxiety
  • Depression and mood swings
  • PTSD-like symptoms
  • Feeling afraid, even at home
  • Hopelessness

If you feel ashamed of your situation or guilty for having any bad feelings about your divorce, please know these responses are completely normal. You didn’t cause your ex’s behavior. You can’t control it—but you can learn how to protect yourself and your kids.

You Need Strategies To Help You Manage Your High-Conflict Divorce

Through therapy, you can learn skills and techniques to stand your ground, recognize manipulation, and protect your wellbeing. Therapy isn’t about getting your ex to change—it’s about empowering yourself.

An experienced therapist can guide you through your situation and help you develop strategies to regain a sense of control. Some of these strategies include:

  • Maintaining minimal communication
  • Setting strong boundaries
  • Limiting your ex’s ability to manipulate you
  • Staying focused on your children
  • Building a life that feels emotionally safe
  • Planning your next chapter

For Court-Mandated Clients

If you were ordered to participate in therapy for co-parenting or custody concerns, you can benefit from working with a therapist who understands the dynamics of high-conflict divorce. We provide evaluations and reports required by the courts, as well as other documentation as needed.

What You’ll Receive In Counseling

  • Therapy sessions focused on personal growth and healing
  • Skills for conflict reduction, boundary-setting, and parenting
  • Documentation for court where required

What You’ll Need for Counseling

  • Commitment to the process
  • Willingness to be honest and self-reflective
  • Openness to developing new skills
